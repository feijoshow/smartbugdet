Student Saving Tips

Food Savings

Meal Sharing

Partner with 2-3 friends to cook and share meals. Cooking larger portions together can save up to 40% on food costs compared to eating out individually. Rotate cooking responsibilities to share the workload.

Smart Grocery Shopping

Shop at Windhoek's most affordable supermarkets (Checkers & Usave) on Wednesday afternoons when student discounts are often available. Plan meals around weekly specials and buy non-perishables in bulk.

Transport Savings

Carpool Schedule

Use the NUST Student Facebook group to arrange regular carpools with students living in your area. Typical savings range from N$200-400/month compared to using taxis or driving alone. Create a WhatsApp group for your regular carpool team.

Alternative Transport

Consider walking or cycling for short distances around campus. NUST's compact layout makes this practical, and you'll save money while getting exercise. Invest in a good bike lock if you choose to cycle.

Study Savings

Textbook Exchange

The NUST library maintains a textbook exchange board where students can buy/sell used books at 50-70% off new prices. Check with your department for subject-specific exchange programs and always ask seniors if they have books to sell or lend.

Smart Printing

Use the NUST library printers instead of commercial shops (saves ~N$0.50/page). Print double-sided whenever possible. Save documents to USB and print multiple items together to minimize trips. Use "draft" mode for personal copies.

Lifestyle Savings

Student Mobile Plans

Switch to a student mobile plan (MTC's Student Bundle or TN Mobile's Student Offer) to save 20-30% on data costs. Use campus WiFi whenever possible and download lecture materials while connected to save mobile data.

Student Discounts

Always ask about student discounts - many Windhoek businesses offer 10-20% off for students (cinemas, restaurants, tech stores). Carry your student ID at all times. Check the NUST student portal for exclusive discount codes.
